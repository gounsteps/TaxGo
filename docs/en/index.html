What is the Lump-sum Withdrawal?

A Right That Expires in 2 Years — Don't Miss It

The Employees' Pension (Kosei Nenkin) and National Pension (Kokumin Nenkin) contributions you paid while working in Japan can be refunded after leaving, provided you meet the eligibility conditions. However, applications are only accepted within 2 years of your departure date — the longer you wait, the more likely you are to lose this right entirely. Communicating directly with Japanese government offices from abroad while preparing the required documents is far from simple. For a fast and reliable refund, using a local agent in Japan is the safest choice.

Eligibility Requirements

Paid pension contributions for at least 6 months

Paid pension contributions for less than 10 years

Within 2 years of leaving Japan

Do not possess Japanese citizenship

No longer have a registered address in Japan

Without a Tax Agent, You Lose 20.42% Forever

When receiving the Employees' Pension Lump-sum Withdrawal, 20.42% is withheld as income tax upfront. This tax can only be refunded by appointing a 'Tax Agent' (Nouzei Kanrinin) residing in Japan — without one, the withheld amount goes directly to the Japanese treasury and cannot be recovered. We handle everything in one stop: appointing your Tax Agent, filing the lump-sum withdrawal claim, and completing the income tax refund.
